## Their Story Together

William Duncombe Major and Virginia Faithful McMaster were Latter-day Saint pioneers who emigrated to Utah Territory in 1863. William's immigration on 3 October 1863 placed him among the thousands of British converts who made the journey to Zion during that decade.

Their daughter Grace Millicent Major, born on 15 September 1870 in Salt Lake City, became a central figure in the Naylor family story when she married Thomas George Naylor. The Major family name—with its distinctive middle name "Duncombe"—suggests English origins, likely from a family with ties to the Duncombe lineage of Yorkshire or another English county. Virginia's surname "McMaster" hints at possible Scottish heritage.

Together William and Virginia raised a family in pioneer Utah that would connect through the Major-Naylor marriage to the broader Taylor-Naylor ancestry that flows down to Erik.
