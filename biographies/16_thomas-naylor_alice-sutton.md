# Thomas Naylor & Alice W. Sutton
## Biography #16

**Relationship to Erik Taylor:** 4× great-grandparents through the Naylor line. Thomas Naylor married Alice Sutton (daughter of John Sutton and Mary Ellison, Biography #17). Their son **Thomas George Naylor** married Grace Major (Biography #10). Thomas George's son Clarence Jordan Naylor married Phoebe Caroline Layton. Clarence's daughter Cynthia Ann Naylor married Byron William Taylor, making Thomas and Alice Naylor the 4× great-grandparents of Erik Taylor.

Line: Erik → Cynthia Naylor → Clarence Jordan Naylor → Thomas George Naylor → **Thomas Naylor & Alice Sutton**

## Thomas: Born to Hardship

Thomas Naylor was born 2 July 1826 in Little Budworth, Cheshire, England — the oldest son of George Naylor and Mary Sefton Naylor. His father George was a blacksmith.

Thomas was only eleven years old when his father left his mother — a devastating blow that left Mary Sefton Naylor to raise five children largely on her own. The older boys including Thomas had to help support the family from an early age.

The Naylor family later moved to Manchester, where they came into contact with the missionaries of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. The family was converted and baptized, a decision that set the entire Naylor line on its course toward Utah.

---

## Alice: A Lancashire Convert

Alice W. Sutton was born 9 February 1837 in St. Helens, Lancashire, England, the daughter of John Sutton — a blacksmith and grocer — and Mary Ellison. She grew up in Parr, St. Helens, Lancashire, in a working-class household.

In 1851, Alice's parents John Sutton and Mary Ellison were baptized members of the LDS Church. The entire Sutton family committed to the faith and to emigration. In February 1853, they sailed on the ship *Elvira Owen* from Liverpool to New Orleans, then made the overland journey to Salt Lake Valley, arriving in October 1853 with Captain Joseph W. Young's company.

Alice came to Utah with her parents at approximately age 16, part of a family caravan that included three wagons, one carriage, two horses, nineteen oxen, and three cows.

---

## Marriage and Pioneer Life

Thomas Naylor and Alice Sutton were married on 16 March 1854 in Salt Lake City — both relatively newly arrived from England, both converts to the LDS faith, both part of the great gathering of Saints to the valleys of the mountains.

Their union produced a family that would become deeply embedded in Utah's pioneer community. Their most notable descendant is **Thomas George Naylor**, born 10 September 1856 in Salt Lake City, who would later marry Grace Major and continue the Naylor line down to Clarence Jordan Naylor, Cynthia Ann Naylor, and ultimately to Erik Taylor.

Other children included:
- **Eliza Naylor** (born 11 January 1871, Salt Lake City) — described in family histories as *"a long and patient sufferer and extremely faithful"*
- **Katherine (Katie) Naylor** (born 23 October 1869, Salt Lake City) — later a hatter by trade

---

## Thomas: Seventies License and Community Faith

Thomas Naylor served as a member of the 22nd Quorum of Seventies in Salt Lake City. A letter preserved in family records, written in 1898 by a family friend to Thomas's missionary nephew Elder Thomas Naylor, reflects on Thomas's legacy of faith: *"The letters received by your mother and sisters, giving me an account of your missionary experiences, and the implicit faith you have…"*

Thomas Naylor died on 7 January 1872 in Salt Lake City at age 45. He left Alice a widow with several young children, including ten-year-old Thomas George Naylor.

---

## Alice: Forty-Five Years of Widowhood

Alice Sutton Naylor outlived her husband by forty-five years. She continued to live in Salt Lake City, where she raised her children and saw grandchildren grow. She died 21 September 1917 in Salt Lake City at approximately 80 years of age — her death certificate recording her residence at 2835 Highland Park Drive, Mill Creek.

Her death certificate notes her parents as John Sutton and Mary Elliston (Ellison), both of England.

Alice was buried in the Salt Lake City Cemetery on 23 September 1917. The undertaker was S.M. Taylor & Co.

---

## Alice's Immigration Record

The Mormon Immigration Index records the Sutton family's crossing:

> **Ship: Elvira Owen | Departure: 15 February 1853, Liverpool | Arrival: 31 March 1853, New Orleans | Arrived Keokuk, Iowa: 13 April 1853**
> 
> Travelers: John, Mary, Anne, Sarah, Ellen, Henry S., Mary, Alice, and Peter Sutton. Origin: Parr Stocks, England.

Alice was approximately 16 on this voyage — one of nine Sutton family members who crossed the Atlantic together.

---

## Legacy in Erik's Line

Thomas Naylor and Alice Sutton stand as an important link in Erik Taylor's ancestry: the Cheshire Naylor line and the Lancashire Sutton line merged in Salt Lake City in 1854, producing Thomas George Naylor, whose descendants eventually led to Clarence Jordan Naylor and Cynthia Ann Naylor (Erik's mother). The chain from Little Budworth and St. Helens to Provo, Utah, to Erik's own birth spans six generations and two centuries.
