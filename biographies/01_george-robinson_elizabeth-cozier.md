## George Robinson

**Born:** 1734, Egg Harbor City, Atlantic County, New Jersey, British Colonial America
**Died:** Date unknown (likely late 18th century, New Jersey)

George Robinson was born in 1734 in the Egg Harbor area of what is now Atlantic County, New Jersey — a region settled largely by Quakers and religious dissenters from England and other parts of the British colonies. The area around Egg Harbor and the surrounding Atlantic and Gloucester County townships was characterized by small farms, pine barrens, and scattered coastal settlements during the colonial period.

Nothing is known of his parents or origins beyond the New Jersey birth record. No military service record connects him to the Revolutionary War, though given his age (roughly 40 when independence was declared in 1776) he would have lived through that period as an adult and landowner. New Jersey was a major theater of conflict during the war, with the region between Philadelphia and New York experiencing repeated military campaigns and occupation.

The primary documentation for George Robinson comes from New Jersey tax lists and census reconstructions, which show a household consistent with a small farmer or tradesman in Gloucester County. He appears on New Jersey tax lists for Galloway Township in the 1790s and 1800s. His will, probated in Essex County in 1876 (through his son John Weldon's estate connection), provides one of the few formal documentary traces.

His daughter **Esther Robinson** (b. 1769, New Jersey) married **John Weldon** (b. 1766, Camden, New Jersey) in 1797 in Gloucester County, New Jersey. It is through this daughter that the Robinson line descends into Erik's family. Their daughter **Abby Jane Weldon** (b. November 7, 1811, Philadelphia) eventually made her way west with the Mormon pioneers, connecting this colonial New Jersey family to the Utah frontier.

## Elizabeth Cozier

**Born:** 1737, Egg Harbor, Gloucester County, New Jersey, British Colonial America
**Died:** 1787

Elizabeth Cozier was born in 1737 in the same Egg Harbor coastal community as George Robinson — very likely in what is now Atlantic or Gloucester County, New Jersey. Her maiden name Cozier (sometimes spelled Cozier, Cossier, or variant forms) suggests possible French Huguenot descent; the name appears in colonial New Jersey records associated with Protestant refugees who fled France following the revocation of the Edict of Nantes in 1685 and settled in the mid-Atlantic colonies.

She died in 1787, likely in New Jersey, at approximately 50 years of age. The exact circumstances of her death are not recorded. She predeceased her husband and would not have lived to see her daughter Esther's marriage in 1797 or the remarkable westward journey her descendants would eventually undertake.

## Their Story Together

George Robinson and Elizabeth Cozier were married in colonial New Jersey, almost certainly in the Egg Harbor or Gloucester County area where both were born and where the Robinson family is documented in subsequent records. Their marriage likely took place sometime in the late 1750s or early 1760s.

They raised their family in the years surrounding the American Revolution — a period of extraordinary disruption for New Jersey families. Their daughter Esther, born in 1769, was seven years old when independence was declared and would have grown up in a household shaped by that conflict.

The connection between this colonial New Jersey family and Erik's heritage passes through a single female line: George and Elizabeth → Esther Robinson → Abby Jane Weldon → Andrew Hunter Scott (her son from the Weldon marriage, who came west with the Mormon pioneers) → a chain of women and men crossing the continent → eventually connecting to the Ward family through which Marian Rula Ward (Erik's grandmother) descends.

## What Is Known; What Is Not

The documentary record for George Robinson and Elizabeth Cozier is thin. No personal letters, diaries, or family histories survive. What we have is primarily:

- Birth and approximate death years from New Jersey records
- Marriage connection established through daughter Esther's records
- Tax list appearances in Gloucester County
- The Millennium File family record connecting George Robinson (b. 1734) and Elizabeth Cozier (b. 1737) as the parents of Esther Robinson who married John Weldon

The 2,290 "memories" attributed to this couple in the FamilySearch record are largely source citations attached across a large collaborative family history project — not personal documents. There are no life sketches, obituaries, or personal accounts.
