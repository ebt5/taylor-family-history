=== Life Sketch ===
Bergite Larsdatter was the daughter of Lars Hanson and Anna Olsdatter (Olsen). She was raised on a farm and knew how to herd cattle, cook, and sew. She preferred the outdoors to housework and would often be left to tend the cattle far from home. She married John Johnson, who in January of 1849, died of a kink in the bowel. leaving Bergite with two daughters, Inger Johnson, age 4 and Lena Johnson, age 2 and a half. Six months after John died, Bergite gave birth to a son in June 1849, and she appropriately named him John Johnson after his recently deceased father.  In 1850 she married Hans Knudsen; together they had these children: Christine, Bertha, Andrew, Herman, and Ellen.

They were introduced to the gospel when Inger brought some missionaries to their home. At first Father Knudson argued with them, but was soon convinced of the truth, and the family began to investigate. Later they were baptized in 1863. They left for Utah in 1864 to worship as they pleased with other members of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints due to harassment from neighbors and other family. They went to Denmark, Germany, Liverpool, and finally crossed the great Atlantic. After which they took rail to Nebraska and attempted to travel by yoke and oxen. They got to near the Missouri River when two of the oxen took ill and died, and they had to leave their belongings near a mail station so they could come back for them after they got to Utah. They walked from the Missouri River to Salt Lake City, where they later learned that Indians had claimed their belongings when they raided the mail station.

## Their Story Together

John Johnson and Bergitte Larsdatter represent the Norwegian stream of pioneer ancestry in Erik's family tree. Bergitte was born on 22 December 1816 in Løken, Elverum, Hedmark, Norway—a small community in the Hedmark region of eastern Norway. Her patronymic surname "Larsdatter" tells us she was the daughter of Lars. She died on 24 August 1915 in Provo, Utah, at the remarkable age of nearly 99.

Bergitte and John were among the Scandinavian converts who joined The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints and made the momentous journey to Utah Territory. According to family records, Bergitte and her descendants (including her daughter Bertha Knudsen Madsen) came to Utah in 1864—part of the great pioneer immigration of that era.

One of the most remarkable facts about Bergitte is that girls were not educated in her early days in Norway, yet she learned to read after she was 70 years of age—a testament to her determination and love of learning. Her son John Johnson (@I24@) married Inger Akkesdotter Sward, continuing the Scandinavian heritage in the family line.

The family records note that Bergitte later was known as "Bergitta Larsen Johnson Knudsen," suggesting she had another relationship or marriage after John Johnson. She lived an extraordinarily long life, witnessing the transformation of Utah from a pioneer territory to a modern state.
