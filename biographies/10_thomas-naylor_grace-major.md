## Their Story Together

Thomas George Naylor and Grace Millicent Major are Erik's direct great-great-grandparents on the Naylor side—the great-grandparents of Erik's mother, Cynthia Ann Naylor. Grace was born on 15 September 1870 in Salt Lake City, Utah, the eldest daughter of William Duncombe Major and Virginia Faithful McMaster. She died on 19 January 1917 in Thatcher, Graham County, Arizona.

Thomas George Naylor was the son of Thomas Naylor (born 2 July 1826 in Little Budworth, Cheshire, England) and continued the Naylor family's proud pioneer heritage. The family eventually settled in Layton, Davis County, Utah, where several of their children were born—their daughter Grace Naomi Naylor (later Merrill Wilson) was born there on 3 May 1897.

The Naylor family connection to England through the elder Thomas Naylor—who was the oldest son of George Naylor—gave this line of the family a distinctly British pioneer heritage, coming through Cheshire. The family had a strong Latter-day Saint tradition, with letters in the GEDCOM records from George Goddard of the Deseret Sunday School Union addressed to "Elder Thomas Naylor" (the uncle), reflecting the family's active role in church service.

Thomas George and Grace's move to Arizona (where Grace died in 1917 in Thatcher) reflected the continued expansion of Latter-day Saint communities into the Southwest in the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries.
