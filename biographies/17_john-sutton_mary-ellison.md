# John Sutton & Mary Ellison
## Biography #17

**Relationship to Erik Taylor:** 5× great-grandparents through the Naylor/Sutton line. Their daughter **Alice Sutton** married Thomas Naylor (Biography #16). Thomas Naylor's son Thomas George Naylor married Grace Major, and their son Clarence Jordan Naylor's daughter Cynthia Ann Naylor married Byron William Taylor — Erik's father.

Line: Erik → Cynthia Naylor → Clarence Jordan Naylor → Thomas George Naylor → Thomas Naylor → Alice Sutton → **John Sutton & Mary Ellison**

## John Sutton
**Born:** 19 January 1788, Billinge, Lancashire, England  
**Died:** 4 March 1865, Nephi, Juab, Utah  
**Parents:** Henry Sutton and Sarah Yates  
**Occupation:** Blacksmith and grocer

## Mary Ellison
**Born:** 13 August 1799, Welsh Whittle (Whittle-le-Woods), Chorley, Lancashire, England  
**Died:** November 1869 (some records say 20 November), Provo or Nephi, Utah  
**Parents:** Adam Ellison and Ellen Glover

**Married:** 23 December 1827, Prescot, Lancashire, England

---

## Lancashire Roots

John Sutton was born 19 January 1788 in Billinge, Lancashire — a mining and farming township near St. Helens. He was the son of Henry Sutton, who held a large farm in the area of Parr, Lancashire. John grew up alongside brothers Peter and Henry (older) and William and Robert (younger), and sisters Ann and Mary.

Henry Sutton's will, made 21 September 1822 and proved June 1823, reflects the family's established position in the community. John learned the trades of blacksmith and grocery, and by the time of his marriage in 1827, he was running a grocery store in Parr, St. Helens.

Mary Ellison was born 13 August 1799 at Welsh Whittle, Chorley, Lancashire — a daughter of Adam Ellison and Ellen Glover. Family histories note that Mary "came from humble beginnings" — so much so that when she married John Sutton on 23 December 1827 at Prescot Parish Church, she could not read or write and signed the marriage certificate with an *"x"*. The marriage register confirms: *"John Sutton has the ability to read and write. Mary Ellison does not."*

John was a widower at the time of their marriage — he had been married previously and lost his first wife. Mary became stepmother to any children from that earlier union, and together they built a new family.

---

## Eight Children in Lancashire

To John and Mary were born eight children, seven of whom lived to adulthood:
1. Ann (born 10 October 1828) — married Charles Langson
2. Sarah (born ca. 1830) — married James Holiday Durney
3. Ellen — married Samuel Linton
4. Mary — married George Goddard
5. **Alice W. Sutton** (born 9 February 1837, St. Helens) — married Thomas Naylor; Erik's ancestor
6. Peter (born 12 July 1839, Parr, St. Helens) — married Mary Duncan Park
7. Henry (died young)
8. Henry John — married Emaline Louise Knowles

The family lived at **220 Parr Stocks, Parr, St. Helens**, where John ran his grocery store. The 1851 Census confirms the family there, with daughter Ann (age 22) helping in the store and Sarah (20) making straw hats. Ellen was living with an uncle, Joseph Greenough, a coal proprietor.

---

## Conversion: January 1851

In January 1851, something changed the Suttons' lives completely.

Mary Ellison Sutton and her older children were baptized members of the LDS Church on **12 January 1851** by John Ashcroft. Mary's brother, James Ellison, joined at the same time. John himself was baptized on **8 March 1851**. At the time of their conversion, John was operating his grocery store and the family was well-established in Parr.

The family's faith was immediate and total. Within two years, they made the extraordinary decision to emigrate to Utah — to gather with the Saints in the valleys of the mountains. John Sutton was ordained a High Priest by Heber C. Kimball in 1855 (according to Endowment House records, he received his endowment in 1854).

---

## The Elvira Owen, 1853: An Epic Crossing

On **15 February 1853**, the Sutton family sailed from Liverpool on the ship **Elvira Owen**, arriving in New Orleans on 31 March 1853. They reached Keokuk, Iowa on 13 April 1853.

John and Mary brought all seven living children. They outfitted themselves for the overland journey with remarkable resources: **three wagons, one carriage, two horses, nineteen oxen, and three cows** — a substantial investment reflecting John's success as a grocer.

They joined Captain Joseph W. Young's company, traveling as an independent detachment using their own means. On 22 September 1853, at the Big Sandy in Wyoming, the company secretary Henry Pugh wrote to President Brigham Young noting the difficulties of lack of grass and daily cattle losses: *"13 gave out on the 20th and 5 or 6 yesterday."*

The Suttons **arrived in Salt Lake Valley in October 1853**.

John Sutton's obituary in the *Deseret News* (1 April 1865) would later record: *"Deceased embraced the gospel in England, in 1851, emigrated to Utah in 1853; was ordained a High Priest in 1857. Brother Sutton assisted many saints to emigrate, and was a very much respected by all who had the pleasure of his acquaintance. He truly lived and died a saint indeed, and leaves a much respected family."*

---

## Life in Utah: Salt Lake, Then Nephi

The Suttons settled first in the 13th Ward in Salt Lake City. John was ordained a High Priest by Heber C. Kimball (one record says Reuben Cahoon and O. Spencer, another says Kimball) in 1855.

After initial settlement, John and Mary moved to **Nephi, Juab County** — a smaller community south of Salt Lake City. John consecrated property there and received a letter from the Church regarding his Consecration in 1854. The family maintained deep roots in the Nephi Ward.

The *Deseret News* of 22 July 1868 published notice of John Sutton's estate, three years after his death.

---

## Deaths in Nephi

John Sutton died 4 March 1865 in Nephi at approximately 77 years of age. His *Deseret News* obituary was published 1 April 1865.

Mary Ellison Sutton followed in November 1869 — her *Deseret News* death notice appearing 15 December 1869. She was approximately 70 years old. Her death certificate records suggest she died in Provo, Utah, though other records indicate Nephi. One old family sheet notes she died in Provo; another says Nephi where "some of their children stayed for many years as farmers."

---

## Alice's Inheritance

Their daughter **Alice W. Sutton** married Thomas Naylor in 1854 and became the mother of Thomas George Naylor, the direct ancestor in Erik's line. Alice's branch carries the Lancashire Sutton heritage — the blacksmith from Billinge, the illiterate but devoted woman from Welsh Whittle, the grocery-store family that gave everything up for faith and emigrated with nineteen oxen and a vision of Zion — all the way down to the Naylor/Taylor line and to Erik Taylor.
