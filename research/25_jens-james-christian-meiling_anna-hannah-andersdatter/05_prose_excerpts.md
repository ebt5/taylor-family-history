## Source: 1902 Manti paper
- **Type:** newspaper death notice / obituary
- **Author:** contemporary newspaper writer/editor
- **Reliability class:** A/B
- **File/Page:** `sources/16_1902__MANTI_PAPER.png`

### Excerpt
> James C. Meiling, one of the pioneer settlers of Mt. Pleasant and a well-known citizen of Sanpete county, died at his home last Wednesday evening, aged sixty-eight years. Mr. Meiling was born in Denmark and came to Utah in 1856, engaging in business at Ephraim, afterward locating at Mt. Pleasant, which has been his home until his death. He was prosperous and was respected as a man of sterling worth and integrity.

**Use:**
- concise contemporary characterization of Jens/James
- confirms public reputation, pioneer status, Ephraim-to-Mount Pleasant movement, and surviving wife/children

**Notes:**
- newspaper says he came to Utah in 1856; compiled biography page says the Copenhagen departure was in April 1857. This conflict needs resolution.
